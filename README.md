<h2 style="color:#1a73e8; border-left:6px solid #ea4335; padding-left:12px;">Ⅰ. Project Core Definition</h2>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
The <span style="color:#ea4335; font-weight:700;">ELEANOR Platform</span> is a next-generation precision biological neural interface system independently developed for axonal neuromodulation, neural repair, and real-time neural activity monitoring. It is built on a dual-module orthogonal architecture, with full localization, embedded integration, cytoplasmic restriction, and zero genomic editing as core design concepts, providing a safe, efficient, and controllable technical solution for translational neuroscience, neurological disease research, and neural injury repair. This platform breaks through the limitations of traditional neuromodulation tools, realizes single-neuron resolution operation, and supports long-term stable monitoring and autonomous self-degradation, filling the technical gap in the field of localized axonal intervention.
</p>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
As a <span style="color:#34a853; font-weight:700;">dual-module integrated platform</span>, ELEANOR strictly separates neuromodulatory functions and reparative functions into two independent signal pathways, avoiding cross-interference between functional modules and ensuring the stability and accuracy of each system. The platform is designed for clinical-grade safety, completely abandoning genomic editing-related technologies, and all regulatory effects are limited to the cytoplasmic space of neural cells, which fundamentally eliminates the risk of gene mutation, insertion, and alteration, and has unparalleled biocompatibility and safety advantages in similar technical systems.
</p>

<h2 style="color:#1a73e8; border-left:6px solid #fbbc05; padding-left:12px;">Ⅲ. Core Architectural Framework</h2>
<h3 style="color:#ea4335; font-size:20px; padding-left:8px;">3.1 Overall Architecture Design</h3>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
ELEANOR v3.0 adopts a <span style="color:#34a853; font-weight:700;">hierarchical orthogonal dual-module architecture</span>, which is divided into two completely independent functional cores: Axonal Neuromodulatory Core and Orthogonal Reparative Core. The two cores use different signal transmission paths and molecular mechanisms, without shared components or cross-activation pathways, ensuring that the operation of one module will not affect the normal function of the other module. The entire platform operates strictly in the cytoplasmic compartment of neural cells, without nuclear localization signals, and does not involve any nuclear-related biological processes, which is the core guarantee of its safety.
</p>

<h3 style="color:#ea4335; font-size:20px; padding-left:8px;">3.2 Module 1: Axonal Neuromodulatory Core</h3>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
This module is responsible for the real-time regulation of axonal electrical signal conduction, including subthreshold excitability tuning, ion concentration homeostasis maintenance, and passive signal collection. It supports <span style="color:#1a73e8; font-weight:700;">BCI read-only signal output</span>, which can capture single-neuron level electrical activity without inputting external stimulation signals to neural circuits, realizing one-way safe data transmission. The module has high temporal and spatial resolution, and can accurately map the activity state of local axonal circuits, providing real-time data support for neural activity analysis.
</p>

<h3 style="color:#ea4335; font-size:20px; padding-left:8px;">3.3 Module 2: Orthogonal Reparative Core</h3>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
This module focuses on the repair and protection of damaged axons, including enhancing axonal membrane stability, activating anti-degenerative signal pathways, amplifying neurotrophic factor effects, and promoting structural regeneration of axons. It is specially designed for neural injury repair scenarios, and can significantly improve the survival rate and functional recovery rate of injured axons. The reparative module and neuromodulatory module run in parallel without interference, and can simultaneously complete neuromodulation and repair tasks in the same cell.
</p>

<hr style="border:1px solid #fbbc05; margin:20px 0;">

<h2 style="color:#1a73e8; border-left:6px solid #673ab7; padding-left:12px;">Ⅳ. Key Technological Characteristics & Safety Advantages</h2>
<h3 style="color:#673ab7; font-size:20px; padding-left:8px;">4.1 Zero Genomic Editing Safety Architecture</h3>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
<span style="color:#ea4335; font-weight:700;">Absolute non-genomic intervention</span> is the core safety design of ELEANOR. The platform does not use any gene editing tools such as CRISPR, TALEN, or ZFN, and does not involve DNA cleavage, base modification, viral vector integration, or exogenous gene expression. All regulatory effects are completed in the cytoplasm, completely eliminating the risk of insertional mutagenesis, off-target genomic effects, and heritable cellular changes. This design makes the platform have clinical-grade safety potential and is suitable for long-term in vivo research.
</p>

<h3 style="color:#673ab7; font-size:20px; padding-left:8px;">4.2 Cytoplasm-Only Targeting Mechanism</h3>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
The molecular structure of the platform is completely restricted to the cytoplasmic space, without nuclear transport function or nuclear localization signals. This design ensures that the platform does not interact with the cell nucleus, does not change the original gene expression profile of neural cells, and maintains the physiological stability of cells. All neuromodulatory and reparative reactions occur in the cytoplasm, with strict subcellular specificity and no off-target effects.
</p>

<h3 style="color:#673ab7; font-size:20px; padding-left:8px;">4.3 BCI Read-Only Signal Interface</h3>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
The built-in BCI read-only system can passively collect axonal electrical signals and output them to external devices, without sending any electrical or chemical stimulation signals to neural circuits. This <span style="color:#34a853; font-weight:700;">one-way signal transmission mode</span> avoids abnormal excitation of neural circuits, ensures the safety of neural activity, and supports seamless connection with mainstream BCI systems for long-term data recording and analysis.
</p>

<h3 style="color:#673ab7; font-size:20px; padding-left:8px;">4.4 KALTSIT Self-Degradation System</h3>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
The platform integrates the proprietary KALTSIT (Kinetic Autonomous Limited-Term Self-Inactivating Termination) system, which can realize programmed autonomous degradation after completing the preset task. The degradation process is non-immunogenic, the byproducts are non-toxic, and there is no residual foreign material in the tissue. This system solves the problem of long-term biocompatibility of implanted neural interfaces and is a key technology for in vivo applications.
</p>

<h3 style="color:#673ab7; font-size:20px; padding-left:8px;">4.5 Single-Neuron Resolution Precision</h3>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
With localized molecular targeting and microdomain restriction technology, ELEANOR realizes <span style="color:#1a73e8; font-weight:700;">single-neuron level operation accuracy</span>, which can modulate and repair individual axons without affecting surrounding normal neural tissues. This ultra-high precision is suitable for the study of fine neural circuits and the development of precision intervention therapies.
</p>

<h3 style="color:#673ab7; font-size:20px; padding-left:8px;">4.6 Real-Time Epilepsy Monitoring System</h3>
<p style="font-size:16px; color:#202124; line-height:1.8;">
The dedicated monitoring module can continuously detect abnormal axonal excitability, epileptiform discharges and pathological oscillations, realize real-time early warning of seizure-like activity, and support long-term tracking of neural activity. This function provides important technical support for epilepsy mechanism research, seizure prediction and neural stability evaluation.
</p>
